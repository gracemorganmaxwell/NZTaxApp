Defintions:

These definitions are based on IRD's definitions for `self-employed` and `contractors`.

Sole Trader or Self Employed:

A sole trader is an individual running their own business. They are the sole owner and are personally responsible for the businessâ€™s debts and obligations. Sole traders declare their business income on their personal tax return, and they pay personal income tax on their business profits.

The term "self-employed" broadly refers to anyone who works for themselves, whether as a sole trader, a contractor or even part of a partnership. Self-employed individuals are responsible for filing their tax returns and paying tax on their income.

Contractor:

Contractors are individuals who provide services to another entity under a contract for services, rather than as an employee. Contractors can work in various fields, ranging from construction to freelance writing. Like sole traders, contractors pay tax on their income through their tax returns, and their tax rates depend on their total income.
